# Ironman 70.3 Periodization & Training Reference

## 1. Training Phases

### Base Building (12-16 weeks)
Build aerobic foundation. Focus on volume, not intensity. Establish movement patterns and build durability for the training blocks ahead.
- Swim: Technique drills, catch-up drill, fingertip drag. Build to 3x/week.
- Bike: Long steady rides at Zone 2. Build weekly long ride to 3+ hours.
- Run: Easy mileage. Build weekly volume 10% per week max. Long run to 90+ min.
- Strength: Max strength phase. Squats, deadlifts, single-leg work. 3x/week.

### Build (8-10 weeks)
Introduce race-specific intensity. Brick sessions (bike-to-run transitions). Increase swim volume.
- Swim: Threshold sets (e.g., 10x100 at T-pace). Open water practice if available.
- Bike: Tempo intervals (2x20 min at FTP 85-95%). Sweet spot work. Brick sessions.
- Run: Tempo runs (20-40 min at half-marathon pace). Intervals (6x800m at 5K pace).
- Strength: Transition to power/endurance. Lighter loads, higher reps. 2x/week.

### Peak (3-4 weeks)
Highest volume weeks. Race simulations. Final fitness gains before taper.
- Swim: Race-pace 1900m time trials. Open water swims with sighting practice.
- Bike: 56-mile simulations at race effort. Nutrition rehearsal on the bike.
- Run: Half-marathon pace runs. Brick runs off 50+ mile rides.
- Strength: Maintenance only. 1-2x/week, light loads.

### Taper (2-3 weeks)
Reduce volume, maintain intensity. Allow body to absorb training and arrive fresh.
- Week 1: Reduce volume 30%. Keep 1-2 intensity sessions.
- Week 2: Reduce volume 50%. One short race-pace effort per discipline.
- Week 3 (race week): See Race Week Checklist below.

### Race Week
Trust the training. No fitness gains possible. Focus on logistics, nutrition, and rest.

## 3. Intensity Distribution

The 80/20 polarized model: 80% of training time in Zone 1-2 (easy/aerobic), 20% in Zone 3+ (tempo/threshold/VO2max).

### How it shifts across phases:
- **Base**: 85/15 or 90/10. Almost all easy. Build the aerobic engine.
- **Build**: 80/20. Introduce structured intervals 2-3x/week.
- **Peak**: 75/25. Highest intensity ratio. Race simulations push into Zone 3-4.
- **Taper**: 80/20 but at reduced volume. Keep a few sharp efforts to maintain fitness.

### Why polarized works:
- Low-intensity training builds mitochondrial density, fat oxidation, cardiac output.
- High-intensity training drives VO2max, lactate threshold, neuromuscular power.
- Moderate intensity (Zone 3 "gray zone") fatigues without driving adaptation. Avoid spending excessive time here.

## 4. Key Workout Types

### Swim
- **Technique drills**: Catch-up, fingertip drag, single-arm, kick drills. 15-20 min per session.
- **Threshold sets**: 10x100m at T-pace (CSS) with 10-15s rest. 5x200m at T-pace with 20s rest.
- **Open water practice**: Sighting every 6-8 strokes. Drafting practice. Beach starts.
- **Race-pace intervals**: 4x500m at goal 1900m pace.

### Bike
- **Long rides**: Zone 2, 2.5-4+ hours. Practice nutrition (aim for 60-90g carbs/hour).
- **Tempo intervals**: 2x20 min at 85-95% FTP. Sweet spot (88-93% FTP).
- **FTP tests**: 20-min test, multiply avg power by 0.95 for FTP estimate.
- **Brick sessions**: 60-90 min ride immediately followed by 15-30 min run at race pace.

### Run
- **Long runs**: Zone 2, 75-120 min. Build to half-marathon distance.
- **Tempo runs**: 20-40 min at half-marathon pace (Zone 3-4).
- **Intervals**: 6-8x800m at 5K pace, 90s jog recovery. 4x1600m at 10K pace.
- **Brick runs**: 15-30 min off the bike. Start at race pace, hold steady.

### Strength
- **Base phase (max strength)**: Back squat 3x5, deadlift 3x5, single-leg Romanian deadlift, pull-ups, planks. Heavy, low rep.
- **Build phase (power/endurance)**: Jump squats, box jumps, kettlebell swings, TRX rows. Moderate load, higher rep.
- **Peak/taper (maintenance)**: Bodyweight circuits, band work, rotator cuff prehab. Light, 1-2x/week.

## 5. Heart Rate Zone Definitions

Based on percentage of maximum heart rate (Max HR):

| Zone | Name | % Max HR | Purpose |
|------|------|----------|---------|
| 1 | Active Recovery | 50-60% | Warm-up, cool-down, recovery days |
| 2 | Aerobic Base | 60-70% | Long steady sessions, fat oxidation |
| 3 | Tempo | 70-80% | Race pace for 70.3, lactate threshold development |
| 4 | Threshold | 80-90% | VO2max intervals, 5K-10K race effort |
| 5 | Anaerobic | 90-100% | Sprint efforts, neuromuscular power, very short duration |

**Estimating Max HR**: 220 - age is unreliable. Better: use highest HR observed in a hard race or all-out interval. Whoop sometimes captures this.

## 6. RPE (Rate of Perceived Exertion) Scale

| RPE | Description | Equivalent |
|-----|-------------|-----------|
| 1 | Very light. Barely moving. | Walking slowly |
| 2 | Light. Comfortable. Could do this for hours. | Easy warm-up |
| 3 | Moderate. Breathing harder but can hold a conversation. | Zone 2 steady |
| 4 | Somewhat hard. Conversation in short sentences. | Upper Zone 2 |
| 5 | Hard. Can speak a few words. | Zone 3 tempo |
| 6 | Harder. Focused effort. Limited speech. | Tempo/threshold border |
| 7 | Very hard. Can sustain for 20-30 min max. | Zone 4 threshold |
| 8 | Extremely hard. 10-15 min max. | VO2max intervals |
| 9 | Near maximal. 3-5 min max. | Hard race finish |
| 10 | Maximum effort. All-out sprint. Seconds only. | Sprint finish |

## 7. Taper Protocol

### Week 1 (3 weeks before race)
- Volume: Reduce 30% from peak week.
- Intensity: Maintain. Keep 2 quality sessions (1 tempo, 1 interval per discipline).
- Long sessions: Shorten by 30%. Last long ride ~3 hours, last long run ~75 min.

### Week 2 (2 weeks before race)
- Volume: Reduce 50% from peak week.
- Intensity: One race-pace effort per discipline. Short and sharp.
- Swim: 1500m at race pace. Bike: 45 min with 2x10 min at race power. Run: 30 min with 2x5 min at race pace.

### Week 3 (Race week)
- See Race Week Checklist. Volume drops to ~20% of peak.

### Common taper mistakes:
- Cutting intensity too much. You lose sharpness. Keep efforts short and fast.
- Adding junk miles from anxiety. Trust the plan.
- Trying new equipment, nutrition, or technique. Race with what you trained with.

## 8. Race Week Checklist

### Sunday (7 days out)
- Last moderate-length workout. 60-min ride + 20-min run at easy pace.
- Confirm race logistics: travel, accommodation, bike transport.

### Monday (6 days out)
- Easy swim 30 min. Technique focus.
- Begin carb loading strategy: increase carbs to 8-10g/kg body weight.

### Tuesday (5 days out)
- Short run 20-30 min with 4x30s strides. Keep legs sharp.
- Lay out race gear. Check bike mechanics.

### Wednesday (4 days out)
- Easy spin 30 min on bike. Shift through all gears.
- Hydration focus: aim for clear urine. Add electrolytes.

### Thursday (3 days out)
- Short swim 20 min. Open water if possible. Practice sighting.
- Rest legs. Light stretching or yoga.

### Friday (2 days out)
- 15-min shakeout ride + 10-min jog. Very easy.
- Attend race briefing. Drive the bike course if possible.
- Prep transition bags. Set up bike if pre-race racking is open.

### Saturday (1 day out)
- 10-min easy swim in race venue water (practice start).
- Check in bike, set up transition area.
- Eat a familiar dinner. High carb, moderate protein, low fiber.
- Set 2 alarms. Prepare race morning breakfast.
- Lights out early. Don't stress if sleep is poor — adrenaline covers it.

### Race Day
- Wake 3 hours before start. Eat breakfast (tested in training): oatmeal, banana, coffee.
- Arrive 90 min before start. Set up transition. Inflate tires. Body glide.
- Warm up: 10-min easy jog + dynamic stretches.
- Swim start: Settle in first 200m. Find your rhythm. Don't go out too fast.

## 9. Nutrition Timing

### Pre-Workout (1-2 hours before)
- 200-400 calories. High carb, low fat, low fiber.
- Examples: Toast with honey, banana, oatmeal, energy bar.
- 16-20 oz water.

### During Workout
- Sessions < 60 min: Water only.
- Sessions 60-90 min: 30-60g carbs/hour (sports drink, gel).
- Sessions > 90 min: 60-90g carbs/hour. Practice this in training.
- Hydrate: 16-24 oz/hour. Add sodium in heat (500-1000mg/hour).

### Post-Workout (within 30 min)
- 20-30g protein + 40-60g carbs.
- Examples: Chocolate milk, protein shake + banana, rice + chicken.
- Rehydrate: 16-24 oz per pound of body weight lost.

### Race Day Nutrition (70.3)
- **Swim**: Nothing. Stay calm, conserve energy.
- **T1**: Sip water. Grab bike nutrition.
- **Bike (56 miles, ~2.5-3 hours)**: 60-80g carbs/hour. Mix of liquid (sports drink) and solid (gels, chews). Take in most calories here — easier to eat on the bike than the run.
- **T2**: Gel or a few sips of cola for caffeine boost.
- **Run (13.1 miles, ~1.5-2.5 hours)**: 30-60g carbs/hour. Gels at aid stations. Cola if stomach allows. Water at every station.

## 10. Common Mistakes

### Overtraining Signs
- Resting heart rate elevated 5+ bpm above baseline for 3+ days.
- HRV declining for a week despite adequate sleep.
- Persistent fatigue that doesn't improve with rest days.
- Loss of motivation, irritability, disrupted sleep.
- Increased injury frequency (tendinitis, stress reactions).
- Performance plateau or regression despite increased training.

### Training Mistakes
- **Too much intensity too early**: Base phase should be 85%+ Zone 1-2. Don't start intervals in week 1.
- **Ignoring recovery data**: Whoop shows red recovery → don't do the interval session. Swap for easy spin.
- **Running too fast on easy days**: Easy runs should feel embarrassingly slow. Zone 2 only.
- **Neglecting swim technique**: Swimming fitness without technique is wasted energy. Drill before volume.
- **Skipping brick sessions**: The bike-to-run transition is unique to triathlon. Practice it every 1-2 weeks in build/peak.
- **Not practicing race nutrition**: Your stomach needs to be trained to handle 60-80g carbs/hour at race effort. If you don't practice, you'll bonk or get GI distress.
- **Comparing to others**: Training plans are individual. Your ACWR, recovery, and volume capacity are unique to you.

### Race Day Mistakes
- Going out too fast in the swim. Settle in, find your rhythm.
- Pushing too hard on the bike. Stick to target power/HR. The run is where races are won or lost.
- Not eating enough on the bike. Front-load your nutrition.
- Trying new gear, nutrition, or pacing on race day. Nothing new on race day.
